# Shopsugiツ — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

A diverse mix of artisan shoppers, art collectors, and gift buyers — united by an appreciation for handmade, unique pieces. They browse with intention, value craftsmanship storytelling, and expect a shopping experience that feels curated rather than transactional.

### Brand Personality

**Artisan · Warm · Refined**

- **Voice**: Welcoming and knowledgeable — like a gallery owner who loves their craft
- **Tone**: Confident but never cold; approachable but never cheap
- **Emotional goal**: Warmth and trust — the customer should feel they're buying from someone who genuinely cares about quality

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone**: Organic warmth with sophisticated restraint — inspired by the Japanese art of Kintsugi (金継ぎ), where broken things are repaired with gold, celebrating imperfection
- **Theme**: Light mode only
- **Color palette**: Kintsugi Gold (`kintsugi-50` → `kintsugi-900`) as the accent, Warm Neutrals (`warm-50` → `warm-900`) as the foundation, plus `cream` and `parchment` specialty tones
- **Typography**: DM Sans for body text (clean, modern), Playfair Display for headings/display (elegant, editorial)
- **Shape language**: Rounded corners (`rounded-2xl`, `rounded-full`), soft shadows, generous whitespace
- **Animations**: Subtle and purposeful — fade-ups for content reveals, micro-interactions on hover, badge pops for cart updates. Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- **Anti-references**: Avoid clinical/cold interfaces, generic e-commerce templates, or anything that feels mass-produced

### Design Principles

1. **Warmth over perfection** — The interface should feel handcrafted and inviting, not sterile. Organic textures, warm tones, and gentle animations over sharp edges and cold precision.

2. **Content is the craft** — Products are the heroes. UI elements should frame and elevate product imagery and descriptions, never compete with them.

3. **Trust through detail** — Thoughtful micro-interactions, consistent spacing, and polished transitions build subconscious trust. Every pixel should feel intentional.

4. **Accessible by default** — WCAG AA compliance minimum. Reduced-motion support, semantic HTML, proper ARIA labels, and sufficient color contrast are non-negotiable foundations, not afterthoughts.

5. **Progressive disclosure** — Don't overwhelm. Reveal complexity gradually. The interface should feel simple on the surface while supporting power-user workflows underneath.
